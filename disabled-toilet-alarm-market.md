<p><p>The disabled toilet alarm market primarily consists of two types: hard-wired and wireless systems. Hard-wired disabled toilet alarms are physically connected to a building's electrical system, providing reliable, constant connectivity and minimizing potential signal interruptions. In contrast, wireless disabled toilet alarms operate using radio frequency signals, allowing for easier installation and flexibility in placement. Both systems are designed to alert caregivers in emergency situations, ensuring the safety and accessibility of facilities for individuals with disabilities.</p></p>
